Common Questions About Freelance Fees

Everything you need to know about platform fees, hidden costs, and maximizing your earnings.

Contra and Toptal charge 0% commission to freelancers, making them the cheapest platforms fee-wise. However, Toptal is extremely selective (top 3% of applicants) and Contra has a smaller client pool. Among mainstream platforms, Guru (5–9%) and Upwork (10%) generally offer better rates than Fiverr (20%) or PeoplePerHour (up to 20%).

Fiverr takes a flat 20% from every order. So if a client pays $100, you receive $80. On top of that, buyers pay a 5.5% service fee, and orders under $75 incur an additional $2.50 small order fee. Withdrawal fees range from free (PayPal) to $3 (Fiverr Revenue Card).

As of 2026, Upwork charges freelancers a flat 10% service fee on all earnings. Clients pay an additional 5% marketplace fee plus up to $4.95 as a contract initiation fee. Freelancers also spend on Connects ($0.15–$0.90 each) to submit proposals, typically needing 4–6 per application.

Start with the project amount, subtract the platform's freelancer service fee, then account for hidden costs like proposal fees (Upwork Connects), small order fees (Fiverr), and withdrawal fees. Use our calculator above to see the exact breakdown for each platform instantly.

Using multiple platforms can be a smart strategy. It diversifies your client sources and lets you optimize based on project type — for example, use a low-fee platform like Contra for larger projects and a high-traffic platform like Upwork for finding new clients. Just be sure to manage your time and profiles effectively.
